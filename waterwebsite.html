WATER

Water is transparent, odourless & tasteless liquid essential for all forms of life. It is abundant on Earth and plays a vital role in various biological & physical processes.

WATER POLLUTION

Water pollution refers to the contamination or degradation of water bodies, such as lakes, rivers, oceans, and groundwater, by various pollutants. It occurs when harmful substances are introduced into the water, making it unfit for its intended use and harmful to the environment and living organisms. Water pollution can originate from various sources, including industrial activities, agricultural practices, improper disposal of waste, sewage and wastewater discharge, oil spills, and chemical runoff from urban areas. These sources introduce pollutants such as chemicals, heavy metals, pesticides, fertilizers, oil, plastics, and biological pathogens into the water. The consequences of water pollution are far-reaching. It poses a significant threat to aquatic ecosystems, leading to the destruction of habitats, loss of biodiversity, and the decline of fish and other aquatic species. Water pollution also affects human health, as contaminated water can spread diseases through consumption or contact. Preventing and mitigating water pollution require collective efforts and the implementation of various strategies. These may include stricter regulations and enforcement, wastewater treatment, proper waste management practices, sustainable agricultural techniques, and public awareness campaigns. Overall, addressing water pollution is crucial for safeguarding the health of ecosystems and ensuring access to clean and safe water for both humans and wildlife.
